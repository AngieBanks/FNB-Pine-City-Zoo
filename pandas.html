PANDAS

The giant panda is a bear species native to China, easily recognized by its distinctive black and white fur. Though classified as a carnivore, its diet is almost entirely bamboo, which makes up about 99% of what it eats. Adult pandas weigh between 100 and 150 kilograms and grow up to 1.5 meters long. They are generally solitary animals, coming together only during mating season.

Habitat

Giant pandas live in mountainous regions with temperate forests, where bamboo is plentiful. They are found at elevations from 1,200 to 3,100 meters.

Population and Conservation

There are roughly 1,864 wild giant pandas today. Thanks to dedicated conservation efforts, their status was improved from "Endangered" to "Vulnerable," though threats like habitat loss and climate change remain.

Behavior and Diet

Pandas spend up to 14 hours a day eating bamboo up to 38 kilograms daily. They are strong swimmers and skilled climbers. Though generally gentle, they can defend themselves if threatened.

Cultural Significance

The giant panda is a beloved symbol of wildlife conservation worldwide and holds special cultural importance in China. It has appeared on coins and was one of the mascots of the 2008 Beijing Olympics.
